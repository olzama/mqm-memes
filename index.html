          What you'll see

          Each item shows an original source-language line and one English translation candidate. Context lines appear below to help orient you. Cultural analysis is available for each item — you don't have to read it, but it can help when the context is unclear.

          Don't read the source language? That's fine — evaluate the English on its own merits: is it funny, fluent, and does it make sense in context?

          How to mark issues

          For each item, mark any issues you notice with a severity and a category. You may mark multiple issues, or none.

          • MAJOR — something significant is lost: the joke doesn't land, the voice feels wrong, meaning is distorted. Even small things — word choice, punctuation — can cause major loss of effect.
          • MINOR — slightly off: less natural or elegant, but the overall effect survives. Example: a joke that lands but feels a bit stiff.

          Category (accuracy, fluency, style, etc.) is for reference only — don't worry if you're unsure which fits.

          A few tips

          • If you judge the same translation the same way 3 times, remaining instances auto-fill.
          • Progress is saved in your browser — you can close the tab and return later.
          • The whole session should take no more than an hour; don't be scared by a large number of items.
          • Some items may show extra or mismatched lines due to segment boundaries — this is normal. Evaluate the part that best matches the original or just evaluate what you see (if you do not understand the original).
